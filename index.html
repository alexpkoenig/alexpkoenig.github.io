A bit about me...

I currently study aerospace engineering and physics at MIT -- after a year or two of being torn between the two fields, I decided to tackle them both. I love space exploration, propulsion systems, experimental aircraft, optical engineering, and telescopes; you'll find examples of my projects and photos in the taskbar above. Outside of school & work, I also enjoy different kinds of exploration -- I'm an astrophotographer, wilderness backpacker, private pilot, cyclist, runner, windsurfer, sailor, SCUBA diver, and climber.

ExoAnalytic Solutions Intern

This fall I'm a full-time intern for ExoAnalytic. My work includes two projects: (1) assisting in developing an architecture for a satellite constellation intended to observe artificial satellites in cislunar space, including the relevant trade studies, cost analyses, and simulations; and (2) creating a high-level design for a company owned-and-built space telescope for providing space situational awareness to customers.

I also interned at ExoAnalytic in Jan 2020. To guide ExoAnalytic's response to the space debris issue in LEO, I created a report recommending guiding features of a future space traffic management architecture. To inform my work, I took inspiration from contemporary efforts in aerospace as a whole (such as UAV integration into the national airspace system), and spoke with leading experts in the space traffic management field.

MIT Rocket Team: Liquid Propulsion Group Team Member

Our primary goal in the liquid propulsion group this semester is to onboard and train freshmen students. I have been developing and executing a propulsion and fluid dynamics curriculum with my team to get incoming freshmen up to speed. I expect to lead a group of 3-4 freshmen in either a theoretical propulsion design project or hands-on building project later this semester (specifics TBD). This semester we are also undergoing design work for new regeneratively-cooled liquid engine, including thermal analysis studies, optimization studies, structural analysis, and fluids system design.

In previous semesters on the liquid propulsion group, we designed and built a 400 lbf. lox+ethanol engine. We have yet to test this system due to the SARS-CoV-2 pandemic, but we hope to as soon as possible. My specific work included envisioning and manufacturing the engine chamber and nozzle; designing, building, and tested ignition system, and creating fluids system and test stand components.
